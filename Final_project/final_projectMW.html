This is Maya Whithaus' Final Project

Growing up with my grandparents allowed me to have a fun music taste. I grew up listening and looking through piles of vinyl records. It’s one of my favorite souvenirs to look for. It’s one of my favorite ways of listening to music. Nothing beats sifting through the paper artwork covers to find the perfect plastic that’ll somehow play your favorite music. I was able to find a good photo to use from Pexels.com, that gives the feeling of being either in an antique store;s record corner or my grandparents’ listening room. The only thing that can really beat a record sound, to me is a live performance. Like the many that are shown at Radio City Music Hall, it’s known for the Rockettes and it’s dazzling shows. The photo used in my animation was a photo I took while taking a pedi-cab through mid-town. I saw some of the performers walk out after a show and I was a little starstuck, so I tried to encapsulate that with the “dazzling” stars. I wanted to also show my own duality with having grown up in a smaller, more quaint structure but having fallen in love with the fast-pace and big show of a city that is New York.
